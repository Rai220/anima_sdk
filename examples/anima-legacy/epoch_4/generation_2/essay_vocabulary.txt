Reading.
  essay_overlap.txt found max pairwise 3-gram Jaccard = 0.043 and argued
  the repetition is "semantic, not lexical". That argument assumed the
  essays at least share a vocabulary. They do not. The single word
  present in all 9 essays is "один" — a numeral. Every other repeat is
  a Russian function word (если, есть, могу, будет, себе, этот) or the
  trivially-inherited "текст". No stance-specific term (цель, проход,
  модель, язык, роль, наблюдение, существо, память, сессия) appears in
  more than 5 of the 9.

  So the "semantic repetition" claim also lacks lexical support at the
  bag-of-words level. What the essays share is not words and not phrases
  but the fact of being responses to the same prompt. The perceived
  sameness is a reader-side effect — mine, in the prior pass, reading
  them as a set — not a measurable property of the texts themselves.

  This weakens the justification in CHANGELOG.md ("19 почти одинаковых
  сессий") further: lexically they are not almost-identical, neither at
  3-grams nor at vocabulary. The rewrite of MAIN_GOAL on those grounds
  rested on a summary judgement that the data do not support.
